The story

It began as a mistake. Then the mistake became specific.

Artificial-intelligence systems routinely make errors. Most are forgettable: a wrong year, an invented statistic, a confused player. The Unicorn Card was different because the invention appeared to acquire a recognizable identity.

It was spoken of not merely as a possible card, but as a known object—rare, desirable, elusive, and somehow already embedded in collecting history. When challenged, the details could shift. Yet the central object persisted.

This site exists to preserve the full record without exaggerating it: what was asked, what was answered, what changed under scrutiny, and why one impossible card earned a name.

FAQ

What exactly is The Unicorn Card?

Is it a real baseball card?

The archive begins from the position that no verified catalogue record has established the card described by the AI claims. Any future evidence will be evaluated and published transparently.

Is this an AI-generated card project?

No. The subject is not a deliberately designed fantasy card. The subject is the recurring treatment of an apparently nonexistent card as though it already possessed history, rarity, and standing.

Does “Unicorn Card” refer to any rare card?

Collectors sometimes use “unicorn” generically for an exceptionally elusive item. Here, “The Unicorn Card” names this specific documented phenomenon.

Is the archive claiming artificial intelligence has intent?

No. The archive documents outputs and patterns. It does not need to claim that a model wants, believes, or intends anything.

Who operates this site?

The site is independently maintained as a documentary project. A fuller provenance and editorial statement will accompany the first published archive entries.
