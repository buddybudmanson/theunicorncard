Why an archive?

Most collectible websites ask what a card is worth.

This archive asks: How did this card become what it is?

The purpose of this website is not to establish a price. It is to preserve the card's documented history, distinguish evidence from interpretation, and make the original sources available to readers.

The central story

Recognition, not declaration

01

Obscurity

The card did not announce itself with a serial number or an obvious rarity stamp. Its distinction had to be noticed.

02

Misidentification

Some examples were reportedly confused with the base card or encapsulated under labels that did not identify the Black parallel.

03

Recognition

Collectors gradually documented the card, explained its identifying features and reconstructed a history that had remained partly hidden.

The surviving evidence

The mistake became part of the record.

A mislabel remains a mistake. But when a Unicorn Card survives inside a holder identifying it as something else, the holder can also preserve physical evidence that the card once passed through the hobby under the wrong identity.

“The mislabel is not the metaphor. The mislabel is the surviving evidence that the metaphor happened.”
