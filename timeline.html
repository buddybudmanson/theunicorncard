Chronology

Timeline of Recognition

A working chronology that should be expanded only when dates and sources can be documented.

  1. 2018

    Release

    Topps issues the Shohei Ohtani FS-5 Future Stars card and its parallels, including the Black parallel.

  2. 2018–2021

    Early obscurity

    The Black parallel receives limited public attention. The precise extent of misidentification during this period remains a subject for documentation.

  3. 2022

    Recorded market history emerges

    Public market databases begin showing identifiable records associated with the Black parallel. Database coverage should not be treated as a complete record of all transactions.

  4. 2024

    Graded sales enter public tracking

    Publicly searchable graded-sale records become easier to locate, while mislabeled examples may remain classified elsewhere.

  5. 2025

    Published cultural recognition

    Victor Wyld includes the Unicorn Card in The Provenance of Desire: The Most Significant Collectibles of All Time.

  6. 2026

    Broader public explanation

    Long-form articles, collector discussions and publicly available identification resources expand awareness of the card and its history.

  7. Present

    A living archive

    New documentation may alter or refine this chronology. Each revision should retain its source and date.

The Unicorn Card Archive
