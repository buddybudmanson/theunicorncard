Definition The Unicorn Card is the 2018 Topps Future Stars FS-5 Shohei Ohtani Black Parallel.

Published record

Published Record

Public sources, dated records and external materials relevant to the history of The Unicorn Card.

Purpose

This page records sources that help establish the card’s identity, chronology, population, public interpretation and place within the history of collecting.

Primary and institutional sources

Topps

2018 Topps Future Stars checklist and issue identity

The manufacturer’s checklist and issue information establish FS-5 as the Shohei Ohtani card within the 2018 Topps Future Stars insert set and distinguish the Black parallel from the base issue.

PSA

Population Report and certification records

PSA’s live databases provide grading-population and certification information. Population figures are snapshots of PSA’s database and do not establish the original print run or total surviving population.

PSA · April 21, 2021

Acquisition of Genamint

PSA announced that it had acquired Genamint, whose machine-learning technology analyzes trading cards in real time, provides diagnostics and measurements, and detects alterations to assist human graders.

The surviving mislabeled Unicorn Card holders are therefore notable in a broader historical sense: the card’s period of misrecognition extended into an era of increasingly sophisticated computer-assisted card analysis. The archive does not claim that a specific AI system personally examined and misidentified any particular slab unless direct evidence establishes that fact.

Archive distinctions

Documented fact
A claim supported by an object, dated record, primary source or verifiable database.
Collector terminology
A name used to describe the card or a class of examples, but not an official manufacturer or grading-company designation.
Interpretation
A conclusion about the meaning or significance of documented evidence.
Correction
A revision made when stronger evidence changes or clarifies the historical record.
