Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to the terms used throughout the archive.

What is the Unicorn Card?

The Unicorn Card is the 2018 Topps Future Stars FS-5 Shohei Ohtani Black Parallel. The name does not refer generally to rare Ohtani cards or to other FS-5 parallels.

What is a “True Unicorn”?

The “True Unicorn” card is the 2018 Topps Future Stars FS-5 Shohei Ohtani Black Parallel still encapsulated in a mislabeled holder. More specifically, the label identifies it as the base FS-5 card, or otherwise fails to identify it as the Black parallel. The phrase is collector terminology, not an official Topps or grading-company designation.

Is the mislabel a mistake?

Yes. The label is incorrect. Its historical importance is that it may preserve physical evidence of the card's earlier misrecognition.

Does a PSA population prove the print run?

No. A grading-company population records cards represented in that company's database. It does not establish the total number originally printed or surviving.

Why link to a live population report?

Population totals can change. Linking to PSA's current report allows readers to inspect the latest available figures rather than relying on a number that may become outdated.

What does “stealth mode” mean?

It is descriptive archive language for a Black parallel that remains hidden within records for another card because it was misidentified or miscatalogued.

Did Topps intend the symbolism?

There is no evidence presented here that Topps intended the card to symbolize Ohtani's career. The metaphor is a later interpretation arising from the card's history.
